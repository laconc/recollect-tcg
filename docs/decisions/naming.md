# Card-naming style guide

The **card-naming register** — a one-screen reference for content authors.
`docs/lore.md` (the Lore Bible) is canon for the world, the factions, and the
voice; this is the quick digest for naming a card. The locked name, domain, and
trademark note live in `docs/decisions/playtest_launch_plan.md` §0.

The sinister Unwritten subset is **the ill intent** (card-kind **`IllIntent`**),
the faction is **the Solace**, and its events are **Unwriting**.

## The register (one line: dry, warm, concrete — never purple, never high-fantasy)

| Layer | Shape | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Legends** | a name like a sentence / an earned title — *"X, Who Y"* or *"X, <title>"* | *Vertigo, Who Loves the Long Fall* · *Roc Paramount, Sky-Entire* · *Home, Who Was a Dog Once* |
| **Plain spirits** | concrete, commonish nouns | *Cloudling* · *Charging Auroch* · *Greyfin Seal* |
| **Unwritten** | *names-that-aren't* — fragments, negations, the half-said | *The Almost-Said* · *Forgotten Name* · *Sentence Fragment* · *The Trailing Off…* |
| **The ill intent** (sinister Unwritten) | menacing / clinical; a first-person menace-sentence is allowed for a boss | *Redacted Bear* · *Page-Eater* · *The Devouring Margin* · *I Too Can Create Desolation* |
| **Solace works** (Unwriting / Rituals) | gentle imperatives and comforts | *A Mercy for the Rim* · *Let It Lie* · *The Soft Close* |

Rules of thumb: prefer a phrase or sentence over a bare abstract noun
("*Desolation*" alone is off-register; *"I Too Can Create Desolation"* is in).
A direct literary quote is acceptable for a Legendary boss, sparing elsewhere.
Keep the vocabulary of the world: spirits are **banished** (never killed); only
the Solace/Unwritten **Unwrite**; banishment leaves an **Impression**, Unwriting
leaves nothing.
